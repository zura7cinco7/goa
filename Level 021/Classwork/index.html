Dog:

Dogs (Canis lupus familiaris) are domesticated mammals that have been companions to humans for thousands of years. They are valued for their loyalty, intelligence, and versatility across a range of roles, from companions to working animals.

Dog Breeds

Working breeds are strong and bred for tasks like guarding, pulling sleds, and search and rescue, including the Rottweiler and Siberian Husky. Herding breeds are agile and intelligent, excelling in managing livestock, such as the Border Collie and Australian Shepherd. Toy breeds are small and often serve as companion animals, like the Chihuahua and Pomeranian. Hounds are skilled hunters with a keen sense of smell, such as the Beagle and Bloodhound. Terriers are energetic and bred for hunting small game or pest control, including the Jack Russell Terrier and Bull Terrier.

Dog Care

A balanced diet with quality dog food is essential. Avoid feeding dogs human foods like chocolate, grapes, and onions, as these can be toxic. Regular physical activity is crucial for maintaining health and preventing behavioral issues, with larger breeds generally requiring more exercise. Grooming includes brushing the coat, trimming nails, and cleaning ears, helping prevent health issues and maintaining comfort.
