How Wi-Fi Was Invented

Wi-Fi was based on radio wave technology. Its origins can be traced back to a project in the 1990s by the Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation (CSIRO) in Australia

They developed a key part of the technology that made fast and reliable wireless communication possible

Wi-Fi is the most popular all over the world

Who Invented Wi-Fi?

Wi-Fi does not have a single inventor but was created through contributions from multiple engineers and scientists

Key Contributor: John O'Sullivan and his team at CSIRO are credited with developing the core technology that underpins Wi-Fi

In nineteen ninety-seven, the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) established the standard (IEEE 802.11) that Wi-Fi operates on

How Does Wi-Fi Work?

Router Sends Signals: The router sends data as electromagnetic waves through the air

Devices Receive Signals: Devices equipped with Wi-Fi adaptors receive these signals and decode them into data

Two-Way Communication: When you send a request (like loading a webpage), your device sends data back to the router, which then fetches the information from the internet.
